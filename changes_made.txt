ENTRY: hodor (node — bio)
ISSUE TYPE: show-only claim / assumption
BEFORE:
Gentle giant whose real name is Walder. Can only say 'Hodor.' Bran wargs into his mind — a deeply transgressive act. His tragic origin, revealed in the show (and presumably the books), connects to Bran's time-altering powers: 'Hold the door.'

AFTER:
Gentle stableboy known as Hodor, whose given name is Walder. In the books he is only able to say 'Hodor.' Bran repeatedly wargs into him, which is portrayed as disturbing and invasive. The 'Hold the door' origin of his name is a reveal from the TV adaptation, not a confirmed event in the published books.

REASON:
"Presumably the books" overstates unconfirmed future content. The 'Hold the door' origin is show-only; the plan requires labeling it as such and not stating it as book fact.

========================================
SUMMARY OF COMMON ISSUES
========================================

Issue types encountered and corrected:

1. SHOW-ONLY STATED AS BOOK FACT
   - Example: Hodor's "Hold the door" origin presented as if it were or would be in the books. Corrected by labeling it as a TV adaptation reveal not confirmed in published books.

2. OVERSTATED CERTAINTY ("secretly," "presumably," "almost certainly," "truly")
   - Nodes: connington (secretly in love), pycelle (secretly loyalist), doran (secretly plotting), balon (almost certainly pushed).
   - Edges: robb->roose, dany->jorah, young_griff->connington, manderly->roose, manderly->ramsay, ilyn->jaime, jeyne_w->robb. Either removed the adverb or rephrased to what the text supports.

3. ASSUMPTION / SPECULATION AS FACT
   - Syrio's death stated as done by Meryn (not confirmed on page); Balon's death as "Faceless Man" (theory); Brienne's descent from Dunk (theory); Harry/Sansa future (future outcome); Howland as "key" to Jon's parentage (speculation); Larys/Lyonel "almost certainly" (softened to "prime suspect" / "suggests").
   - Jon's parentage and R+L=J: kept as "strongly implied" or "if R+L=J is true" where appropriate.

4. FAN THEORY OR READER SPECULATION IN REFERENCE TEXT
   - ned->benjen "some fans speculate" removed in favor of "reasons not stated in the books."
   - duncan_tall: Brienne descent labeled as "some readers suggest ... not confirmed."
   - jon->dany: "promised convergence" changed to "often anticipated by readers."

5. WORDING CLEANUP
   - jon bio: separated show-confirmed from book-implied for parentage.
   - harry: future outcomes rephrased to plans and conditionals.
   - meryn/syrio (node and edges): Syrio's fate described as ambiguous rather than assumed death.

Counts:
- Node fields changed: 15 (bios; no title or bookNote changes in this pass beyond what was needed for accuracy).
- Edge rels changed: 12.
- Nodes reviewed with no change: 181 (after excluding the 15 node bios edited).
- Edges reviewed with no change: 481 (after excluding the 12 edge rels edited).

The dataset now presents a more cautious, canon-aware reference: show-only content is labeled, speculation is flagged or rephrased, and certainty language is reduced to what the published text supports.
