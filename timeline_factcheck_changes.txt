TIMELINE FACT-CHECK CHANGELOG
=============================
Date: 2026-03-11
Scope: All 9 timeline eras in src/App.jsx (lines 2100–2313)
Method: Line-by-line verification against published book canon (ASOIAF novels, TWOIAF, Fire & Blood) and confirmed TV canon (Game of Thrones S1–S8).

14 entries corrected. 0 entries fabricated from scratch.
Entries verified as accurate were left unchanged.

CHANGE 1 — Dawn Age Summary (Era 1)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIELD: summary
LOCATION: Era "The Dawn Age & The Long Night"

BEFORE:
"The last hero, wielding dragonglass, allied with the Children to defeat them."

AFTER:
"The last hero sought out the Children of the Forest and together they drove back the darkness."

WHY:
The Last Hero is never described as wielding dragonglass in Old Nan's tale (AGOT, Bran IV). His SWORD froze and the steel shattered. Dragonglass (obsidian) is associated with the Children of the Forest and with killing Others (discovered by Sam in ASOS), but the Last Hero legend does not attribute dragonglass to him specifically. This was an incorrect conflation.

CHANGE 2 — The Last Hero's Blade (Era 1)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIELD: actions (character: The Last Hero)

BEFORE:
"His dragonglass blade shattered, but he found the Children and together they drove back the darkness."

AFTER:
"His sword froze so hard the steel shattered, but he found the Children and together they drove back the darkness."

WHY:
Old Nan's story (AGOT, Bran IV): "his sword froze so hard the steel shattered." It was a steel blade, not dragonglass. The "horse" was also missing from the list of companions lost. Added "his horse" alongside companions and dog for completeness.

CHANGE 3 — Azor Ahai / Prince That Was Promised (Era 1)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIELD: actions (character: Azor Ahai)

BEFORE:
"Prophecy holds he will be reborn as The Prince That Was Promised."

AFTER:
"Some prophecies suggest he will be reborn; Melisandre equates him with The Prince That Was Promised, though the texts do not confirm they are the same figure."

WHY:
The books do NOT definitively state Azor Ahai and The Prince That Was Promised are the same person. Melisandre conflates them, but Maester Aemon and other characters treat them as potentially separate prophecies from different traditions (Asshai vs. Valyrian). Presenting them as identical is an assumption. Conservative phrasing applied.

CHANGE 4 — Robert's Rebellion Summary (Era 4)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIELD: summary

BEFORE:
"the Mad King burned Brandon and Rickard Stark alive"

AFTER:
"the Mad King executed Rickard and Brandon Stark — burning Rickard alive in his armor while Brandon strangled himself trying to save his father"

WHY:
Factually wrong. Rickard Stark was burned alive inside his armor using wildfire. Brandon Stark was placed in a strangulation device (a Tyroshi device with a leather cord) and strangled himself trying to reach a longsword placed just out of reach, while watching his father burn (ASOS, Jaime's confession to Brienne). Brandon was NOT burned. This is a well-established detail repeated multiple times in the text. Saying both were "burned alive" is incorrect.

Also changed "absconded with" to "took" — "absconded" implies theft/criminality. The books deliberately leave ambiguous whether Lyanna went willingly or was abducted. Conservative phrasing.

CHANGE 5 — Theon Greyjoy in AGOT (Era 5, Book 1)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIELD: actions (character: Theon Greyjoy)

BEFORE:
"Robb's companion, sent to the Iron Islands as envoy. Torn between identities."

AFTER:
"Robb's companion and ward of the Starks. Rides south with Robb when the banners are called."

WHY:
Wrong book. Theon is NOT sent to the Iron Islands in A Game of Thrones. That happens in A Clash of Kings (ACOK), when Robb sends him to treat with Balon Greyjoy. In AGOT, Theon is at Winterfell and then accompanies Robb south. This was a chronological error — an ACOK event placed in the AGOT section.

CHANGE 6 — Tywin at the Whispering Wood (Era 5, Book 1)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIELD: actions (character: Tywin Lannister)

BEFORE:
"Unleashes Gregor on the Riverlands. Outmaneuvered by Robb at the Whispering Wood."

AFTER:
"Unleashes Gregor on the Riverlands. Tricked by Robb into engaging a decoy force at the Green Fork while the main Stark host captures Jaime at the Whispering Wood."

WHY:
Tywin was NOT at the Whispering Wood. Jaime Lannister was at the Whispering Wood (besieging Riverrun). Tywin was at the Green Fork, where he fought Roose Bolton's decoy force. Robb's strategy was to split his army — sending a smaller force under Bolton to occupy Tywin at the Green Fork while Robb's cavalry surprised Jaime at the Whispering Wood. Saying Tywin was "outmaneuvered at the Whispering Wood" confuses two separate battles.

CHANGE 7 — Renly Baratheon in AGOT (Era 5, Book 1)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

BEFORE:
"Gathers the south to his banner despite no legal claim."

"Flees King's Landing after Robert's death, refusing to support either Joffrey or Stannis."

CHANGE 9 — Jon Snow Sequence in ACOK (Era 6, Book 2)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIELD: actions (character: Jon Snow)

BEFORE:
"Scouts the Skirling Pass. Kills Qhorin to infiltrate wildlings. Meets Ygritte."

AFTER:
"Scouts the Skirling Pass. Captures Ygritte but lets her go. Kills Qhorin Halfhand on Qhorin's orders to infiltrate the wildlings."

WHY:
Events listed in wrong order. Jon meets and captures Ygritte BEFORE killing Qhorin Halfhand, not after. The actual sequence: Jon goes on the Great Ranging → scouts the Frostfangs/Skirling Pass → captures Ygritte (and is ordered to kill her but lets her go) → the party is hunted by wildlings → Qhorin orders Jon to kill him to gain the wildlings' trust → Jon kills Qhorin and infiltrates Mance's host. The original entry reversed the Ygritte and Qhorin events.

CHANGE 10 — Tyrion's Injury at the Blackwater (Era 6, Book 2)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIELD: actions + added showNote (character: Tyrion Lannister)

BEFORE:
"Slashed across the face."

AFTER:
"Gravely wounded in the battle." + showNote: "In the books, Tyrion loses most of his nose. The show gives him a facial scar instead."

WHY:
"Slashed across the face" describes the show version. In the books (ASOS, Tyrion's recovery chapters), Tyrion loses most of his nose — a far more disfiguring wound. This is a significant book/show divergence that should be noted rather than silently using one version. Added a showNote to separate the two canons cleanly.

CHANGE 11 — Cersei's Walk of Atonement (Era 8, Book 4)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIELD: actions + added showNote (character: Cersei Lannister)

BEFORE:
"Arms the Faith Militant. Frames Margaery. Gets arrested for her own sins. Walk of atonement."

AFTER:
"Arms the Faith Militant. Frames Margaery. Gets arrested for her own sins." + showNote: "The Walk of Atonement occurs in her ADWD chapters, since AFFC and ADWD are concurrent."

WHY:
The Walk of Atonement does NOT occur in A Feast for Crows. It occurs in Cersei's chapters in A Dance with Dragons (Cersei II, ADWD). AFFC and ADWD cover the same time period but are split by geography/POV. Cersei's arrest is in AFFC; her Walk is in ADWD. Listing the Walk under AFFC is a misattribution. Moved the Walk to a showNote clarifying the actual book placement.

CHANGE 13 — Wyman Manderly's Frey Pies (Era 9, Book 5)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIELD: actions (character: Wyman Manderly)

BEFORE:
"Fed the Freys to the Boltons in pies."

AFTER:
"Baked three missing Freys into pies and served them at the Winterfell feast."

WHY:
The original phrasing is misleading. Manderly did not "feed the Freys to the Boltons" — he baked three Frey men (Rhaegar, Symond, and Jared Frey) into meat pies and served them at the Winterfell harvest feast to everyone present, including Boltons, Freys, and other guests. The pies were served openly at a feast, not targeted at the Boltons specifically. Also expanded the "North remembers" quote to include its fuller context from Manderly's speech to Davos (ADWD, Davos IV).
